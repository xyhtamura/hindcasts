effects that have read to the end before they begin.

A live effect is causal — its output can only react, late, to evidence already past. Hindcasts drops that constraint on purpose: each instrument works with the whole static file in hand, so it can respond before an event, smooth backward, and fit its curves to the entire piece at once. sounder sounds the depth · pythia speaks ahead of knowing · prolepsis blooms before the hit

A live effect is causal: its output at time t can depend only on samples at or before t. A live compressor waits for evidence a peak arrived, then responds — always behind. That reliance on realtime is built into most audio effects (less so video), yet the digital medium does not have to work this way. That single constraint is causality, and it forces the threshold-and-attack dance, the running estimate, the late action.

Hindcasts drops causality on purpose. These effects work with full knowledge of the static file — so they can respond before, smooth backward, and fit their transfer curves to the entire piece at once.

It takes offline processing to its logical conclusion. Audio is causal-by-default; image is acausal-by-default. Hindcasts lets audio feel the way image processing already works in digital space, and refuses the inertia of that hidden commitment to realtime.

The five principles of acausality

what offline unlocks
①Precognition
Respond before. Pre-ducking, pre-verb, the tail that precedes the hit.
②Global statistics
Shape relative to the whole file's distribution, not a running estimate.
③Bidirectionality
Smooth and filter symmetrically, not only forward. Zero-phase.
④Multi-pass
Measure, then act, then measure again. Convergence no live system gets.
⑤Whole-signal optimization
Pick the curve / quantizer / kernel optimal over the entire piece.
